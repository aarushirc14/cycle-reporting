Forecast Movement

What Changed Since April 29

Leading indicator movement since previous forecast

What Changed

  • Made appointments shifted into a steeper downward trajectory.
  • Prequalified leads declined to mid-to-low levels.
  • Total lead generation softened, including OSC and call-in sources.
  • Organic session duration reached an annual peak, offsetting some paid-duration weakness.

What Did Not Change

  • Held appointments remain relatively high and stable.
  • Overall web traffic remains stable, with a slight month-over-month increase.
  • The May 13 forecast baseline remains about 72 homes over 90 days, or 24 homes/month.

How It Changes the Review

  • Start with targeted community reviews instead of a blanket traffic push.
  • Community Pulse flags traffic deficits at Lake County Communities, Lake Deer Estates, Stillwater Cove, and Deland.
  • The Reserve At Hammock Oaks still needs a conversion-quality review because traffic exists but web-to-lead performance is weak.
